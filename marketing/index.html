A minimalist Gmail client built for spotty connections.

Airplane wifi. Rural networks. Developing regions. Anywhere Gmail's multi-megabyte interface refuses to load — BAREMAIL gets your email through in kilobytes.

── why BAREMAIL ──
⚡
Tiny app shell
The entire UI is under 60KB gzipped. Cached by a service worker on first visit — zero bytes on every visit after.
✈
Offline-first
Read cached emails with no connection. Compose and queue messages in the outbox — they send automatically when you're back online.
🌐
Zero install
Works in any modern browser. Install as a PWA to launch from your dock — no app store, no downloads.
🔒
Privacy-first
No backend server. Your emails go directly between your browser and Google's Gmail API. No data ever touches a third party.
⌨
Keyboard-driven
Navigate with j/k, open with o, compose with c, reply with r, archive with e. Vim-style flow for power users.
◐
Light + Dark
Respects your system preference. Toggle anytime. The amber terminal aesthetic looks great either way.
BAREMAIL is open source
MIT licensed. Self-host it, fork it, contribute to it. The entire codebase is designed to stay small, readable, and dependency-light. Every PR must keep the app shell under 200KB.
